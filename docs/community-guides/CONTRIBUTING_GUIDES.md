# Contributing a Community Travel Guide

Anyone signed in to Incredible India Explorer can write a destination guide.
This page walks through the workflow end to end.

## 1. Sign in

Guides are tied to your account, so [sign in](../../login.html) first (email/password
or Google, same as the rest of the site).

## 2. Write your guide

Go to **Explore More → 📝 Community Guides → + Write a guide**, or open
`guide-editor.html` directly.

- **Destination** — the place your guide is about (e.g. "Jaipur, Rajasthan").
- **Title** — a short, specific title. "A first-timer's guide to the Pink City"
  beats "Jaipur guide".
- **Content** — use the toolbar for bold, italics, and lists. Write in your own
  words; cite prices/hours as of the date you're writing, since these change.

You can click **Save as draft** at any time — drafts are only visible to you
and are not reviewed until you submit them.

## 3. Submit for review

Click **Submit for review** when you're happy with the guide. This moves it
into the moderation queue (see [MODERATION.md](./MODERATION.md)) — it is not
public yet.

## 4. What happens next

- **Approved** → your guide becomes visible to everyone on the
  [Community Guides](../../community-guides.html) page.
- **Rejected** → you'll see a reviewer note explaining why. Edit the guide and
  resubmit — rejected guides can always be resubmitted.

## 5. Editing a published guide

Edits to a published guide don't overwrite it immediately. Editing creates a
new version and puts the guide back into the review queue; the version that's
currently public keeps showing until your edit is approved. This keeps public
guides from silently changing without review, while still letting you fix
mistakes right away.

## 6. Version history

Every save (draft, submission, or edit) creates a new version. From a guide's
detail page, authors and moderators can open **Version history** to see every
past version and **restore** an older one if a recent edit made things worse.
Restoring by a regular author re-submits for review; restoring by a moderator
publishes immediately.

## 7. Rating and reporting

Any signed-in user can rate a published guide 1–5 stars, or report a guide
that's outdated, inaccurate, spammy, or offensive. Reports go straight to the
moderation queue — see [MODERATION.md](./MODERATION.md).

## Good guide etiquette

- Write from firsthand experience where you can.
- Call out things that change often (entry fees, opening hours, seasonal
  closures) so future readers know to double-check.
- Don't copy text from other websites, guidebooks, or Wikipedia — write it in
  your own words.
